# Logic of Cluster-Based Analysis

## 1. Rationale for Clustering

Cluster-based analysis is used to identify clinically meaningful
configurations of personality and affective functioning that are not
adequately captured by single-variable or linear models.

The goal is to move beyond symptom severity toward structural patterns.

---

## 2. Conceptual Foundation

Clustering is grounded in the conceptual framework of affective instability
as a central organizing feature of emotional maladaptation.

Personality traits and affective indicators are treated as interrelated
dimensions forming coherent profiles rather than independent predictors.

---

## 3. Clinical Interpretability

Clusters are evaluated not only by statistical criteria, but by:

- Internal coherence
- Clinical plausibility
- Differentiation between profiles
- Relevance to psychotherapy practice

This dual criterion ensures practice-based validity.

---

## 4. Methodological Principles

- Exploratory, data-driven approach
- Use of standardized variables
- Preference for hierarchical and variance-based methods
- Emphasis on replicable profile structures

---

## 5. Role in the Research Program

Cluster analysis serves as a bridge between:

- Conceptual models
- Empirical findings
- Theory-building efforts

It provides a structural map for subsequent analyses and publications.
