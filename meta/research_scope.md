# Research Scope

## 1. Core Research Focus

This research program investigates affective instability as a central
psychological construct underlying emotional maladaptation in adult
psychotherapy clients.

The focus is on personality structure, affect regulation patterns,
and clinically meaningful configurations observed in practice-based data.

---

## 2. Population and Context

- Adult psychotherapy clients (outpatient settings)
- Practice-based clinical contexts
- Non-acute, non-inpatient populations

---

## 3. Conceptual Boundaries

This research program explicitly includes:

- Affective instability
- Personality organization and traits
- Emotional maladaptation
- Practice-based and clinically grounded models

This research program explicitly excludes:

- Biological or genetic mechanisms
- Pharmacological efficacy studies
- Acute psychiatric populations
- Child and adolescent samples

---

## 4. Methodological Orientation

- Practice-based research
- Quantitative and mixed-method approaches
- Emphasis on clinically interpretable patterns
- Exploratory and theory-building analyses

---

## 5. Intended Academic Trajectory

This repository serves as a cumulative research environment supporting
progression toward doctoral-level research and peer-reviewed publication.
